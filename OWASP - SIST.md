# Introduction
The **OWASP Sathyabama Institute of Science and Technology – Student Chapter** is an official student branch of the **OWASP Foundation**, dedicated to promoting application security and secure software development practices within the campus and beyond.  
We bring together students, faculty, and industry professionals to build a vibrant community around cybersecurity, web application security, and modern threat‑landscape awareness. Our chapter leverages the open‑source resources and projects hosted by OWASP to conduct hands‑on sessions, workshops, and awareness events that help members understand and mitigate real‑world security risks.

# What We Do?
1. Knowledge‑sharing sessions and workshops:
	- We conduct workshops on topics such as the **OWASP Top 10**, secure coding practices, web application penetration testing, and API security, helping students build a strong foundation in application security. 

2. Hands‑on labs and CTF‑style activities:
	-  The chapter organizes practical labs and Capture‑The‑Flag‑style exercises to simulate real‑world attack and defense scenarios, enabling participants to apply security concepts in a controlled environment.

3. Community building and collaboration:
	- We foster an open and inclusive environment where students can collaborate on projects, contribute to OWASP documentation, and share research or blog posts related to application security.

4. Industry interaction and career guidance:
	- The chapter invites guest speakers from the industry, security researchers, and alumni to share insights on penetration testing, bug‑bounty programs, and career opportunities in cybersecurity.

# How OWASP is different from others
1.  Open-Source and community driven:
	- OWASP is a non‑profit, global community that publishes open‑source tools, documentation, and standards (like the **OWASP Top 10**, Testing Guide, and Development Guide) that are freely available to anyone, unlike many commercial security frameworks.

2. Vendor‑neutral and independent:
	- OWASP does not endorse any specific product or vendor; its content is created and reviewed by a global community of practitioners, ensuring unbiased guidance and best practices for application security.

3. Focus on practical, real‑world security:  
    - Instead of only theoretical compliance, OWASP emphasizes practical testing methodologies, threat modeling, and risk‑based security that align well with real‑world projects and development workflows.
    
 4. Global reach with local chapters:
	- OWASP empowers local chapters like ours to adapt global resources to local needs, enabling campus‑level events, student‑driven projects, and community‑specific security initiatives.
